1 — Net Read

The drug is very unlikely to be causing these cancers — but both the company and the loudest bulls are over-stating the certainty, and the reassurance rests on (a) a background benchmark skewed high and (b) data that pool away a real 50 mg-concentrated signal.

  1. Biology strongly favours "not drug-caused." Obefazimod's sole defined action is up-regulating miR-124, a tumour suppressor silenced across cancers — mechanistically the opposite of a carcinogen; it is not a classical immunosuppressant and not a kinase inhibitor, so the JAK-class malignancy rationale does not transfer; and the latencies are far too short — invasive breast (≈6.8 mo) and Gleason-7 prostate (≈8.5 mo) cancers were almost certainly subclinical at enrolment and screen-detected.
  2. But "within background" is built on an inflated yardstick. Abivax's derived ranges (NMSC 0.7–1.4; non-NMSC 0.3–0.7 / 100 PY) are anchored on immunosuppressant-treated IBD cohorts — the NMSC ceiling (1.36) is literally the 5th cumulative year of thiopurine exposure — plus a "5× prostate" figure that is not in the cited paper and a breast prevalence passed off as incidence. A mechanism-matched UC baseline is closer to NMSC ~0.4–0.9 / non-NMSC ~0.3–0.5.
  3. My independent recalculation shows the 50 mg arm runs above even those inflated ceilings (maintenance-matched NMSC 2.82 vs 1.4; non-NMSC 1.41 vs 0.7 / 100 PY), with all non-NMSC malignancies and 4 of 5 obefazimod NMSC cases in the 50 mg arm — a dose-response is a classic causal fingerprint. The decisive caveat is statistical, not exculpatory: with 2–4 events the 95% CIs are enormous and overlap background, so nothing is conclusive either way. The 25 mg arm is clean.
  4. The circulating bull "pooled" sheet reaches the right base case but is a synthesis, not a primary source. About half traces to real disclosures (the deck; the 22 May Study 108 6-K); it uses optimistic 100%-completion patient-years and a 25 mg dose-attribution that flatters. Update (15 Jun): the ABX464-104 protocol (NCT04023396) now verifies the sheet's genotoxicity rows (Ames/micronucleus negative) to a primary source — but not the carcinogenicity bioassays — and reveals two items the bulls omit: a phototoxic metabolite (a drug-specific mechanism for the NMSC cases) and the sponsor's own pre-specified malignancy AESI citing a long-term immune/MoA concern. So "no pathway to cancer" is too strong: initiation (genotoxicity) is reasonably excluded, but a local skin-promotion route exists.

Verdict on the sell-off: an over-reaction to small numbers in a mechanistically-implausible carcinogen — but not baseless, because the 50 mg dose-concentration is real and the genuine open risk (unpublished long-term + preclinical carcinogenicity data) is unresolved. Probability of a malignancy boxed warning: low (obe is neither a JAK nor an anti-TNF, the two UC classes that carry one) — but "no boxed warning" is the likely, not the certain, outcome. The end-Q2 Part 2 + Phase 2 combined exposure-adjusted readout is the swing factor.

Commentary. Every non-NMSC malignancy and four of five obefazimod NMSC cases are in the 50 mg arm, which also carries the highest overall/serious TEAE burden. But the 50 mg cohort was also the most heavily pre-treated (prior advanced-therapy failure 46.2% vs 39.4% vs 37.5% [Deck 1 p.9]) and had the most follow-up (82.1% completion vs 34.3% placebo) — both independently raise observed cancer counts. That confound is the spine of this entire analysis: more drug exposure ≠ more carcinogenesis if it also means more screening time in a higher-risk population.

3 — Independent patient-year recalculation

Method: Deck 1 p.14 discloses headache incidence rates per 100 PY next to subject counts, so maintenance PY backs out exactly — PY = subjects ÷ (rate/100). This is more reliable than assuming a mean exposure, and it is the audited basis for every rate below (sources/py_recalc.py).

Adding the 8-week induction (obefazimod in both obe arms) gives total obefazimod exposure ≈164.4 PY (25 mg), ≈171.5 PY (50 mg), ≈335.9 PY combined — the denominator Abivax will use for "exposure-adjusted by dose." Verdict on the benchmark. The quoted numbers are mostly real and mostly transcribed correctly (3 citation errors, 1 fabricated-looking "5×"), but the derived ranges are advocacy, not neutral epidemiology: the high ends rest on thiopurine/anti-TNF-treated populations whose excess risk comes from a mechanism obe does not share, while the company's own best population-based citations (Jess; Carli UC RR 1.22 NS) point to background-level risk. A fairer, mechanism-matched window is roughly NMSC 0.4–0.9 and non-NMSC 0.3–0.5 / 100 PY — against which the 50 mg point estimates look worse, not better. The honest takeaway is not "the benchmark proves safety" but "the event counts are too small to support any firm rate conclusion."

Why these are unlikely drug-related

1Latency far too short. Invasive Grade-2 breast (6.8 mo) and Gleason-7 prostate (8.5 mo) cannot arise de novo in months — subclinical at enrolment, screen-detected.
2Established risk factors in nearly every case — age ≥60 (most), prior skin cancer (cases 2,4,5), and prior thiopurine / JAK exposure (cases 4,5,7) — i.e. the very agents that carry NMSC/malignancy warnings.
3No coherent histology — BCC, SCC, ductal breast, prostate adeno; a true carcinogen usually yields a consistent tumour type. Most rated Not/Unlikely.
4Differential follow-up — obe patients stayed on study ~50% longer than placebo (82% vs 34%) → more screening visits → more incidental detection, independent of true incidence.

Residual reasons for caution

1Dose-response. All non-NMSC malignancies and 4/5 obe NMSC cases in 50 mg; 25 mg had zero non-NMSC. A monotonic gradient is a causal fingerprint (counter: tracks heavier pre-treatment & longer exposure).
2Two SCCs rated "Possibly Related" — not fully dismissed by investigators.
350 mg point estimates exceed even the inflated benchmark for both categories.
4Phototoxic metabolite (§8). The protocol confirms ABX464-N-Glu is phototoxic at clinical exposure — a plausible drug-specific promotion mechanism for the NMSC (skin) cases, which are 5 of 8 events and 50 mg-skewed.
5Sponsor pre-flagged malignancy as an AESI on a long-term immune/MoA concern; carcinogenicity bioassays still unconfirmed. Mechanistic implausibility ≠ proven human safety.

On the dysplasia case: colonic dysplasia is a pre-malignant UC surveillance finding, not cancer; UC patients on surveillance have meaningful background dysplasia-detection rates. Counting it as a "cancer" overstates the tally — correctly excluded from the malignancy rates above.

8 — Mechanism: carcinogenesis pathways (now partly primary-sourced)

There are two principal routes by which a drug causes cancer in humans: (1) genotoxicity (direct DNA damage / mutation — initiation) and (2) non-genotoxic promotion, dominated for immunology drugs by immunosuppression. The circulating bull thesis argues obe fails both. A primary source obtained 15 Jun 2026 — the ABX464-104 Phase 2b maintenance protocol (V5.1, 11 Jul 2022; clinicaltrials.gov NCT04023396, §1.2.4), saved in sources/ — now resolves much of this, and surfaces a third, skin-specific mechanism the bulls omitted.

Pathway 1 — Genotoxicity: genotox CLEARED (now sourced), carcinogenicity bioassays still unconfirmed

The protocol confirms the genotoxicity rows of the bull sheet to a primary source:

ABX464-104 protocol §1.2.4 — verbatim
"ABX464 was found to be non-genotoxic. Its main metabolite, the ABX464-N-Glu was not mutagenic as assessed by an Ames test, an in vitro and an in vivo micronucleus assay."

verified Ames, in-vitro and in-vivo micronucleus all negative; N-Glu non-mutagenic. But the carcinogenicity bioassays remain unverified: this protocol lists tox durations only "2-weeks to 6/9 months" and makes no mention of a 2-year rat carcinogenicity or 6-month TgRasH2 mouse study — so the bull sheet's "2-yr rat / TgRasH2 CLEARED" rows still lack any traceable source (those long-term bioassays are typically run pre-NDA; status unknown). Net: initiation via direct mutagenesis is now reasonably excluded; the formal lifetime carcinogenicity readouts are not yet in hand.

Pathway 2 — Immunosuppression: clinically clean, but one real nonclinical flag

Clinical data point away from immunosuppression: serious/severe infections 0.5% (50 mg) vs 1.0% (placebo), zero opportunistic infections, no herpes-zoster signal [Deck 1 p.13]; Phase 2b lymphocyte and CD8/NK counts preserved (unlike JAKs). However, the protocol confirms a genuine preclinical immunosuppression signal — not just a "bull-sheet caveat":

ABX464-104 protocol — verbatim
"Immunosuppression has been observed during the 9-month cynomolgus monkey study with ABX464-N-Glu dosed directly by oral gavage."

It did not translate to a clinical infection signal at therapeutic exposure, but it means "no immunosuppression pathway" is overstated — the metabolite is immunosuppressive at sustained high exposure, which is exactly the long-term concern for malignancy surveillance.

Pathway 3 — Phototoxicity: a drug-specific, NMSC-relevant mechanism the bulls ignore

🔴 New material finding. The same protocol discloses: "oral administration of ABX464-N-Glu was phototoxic in a nonclinical model at clinically relevant exposure levels" — and subjects are formally counselled on sun protection. Phototoxicity / photosensitisation is a recognised, non-genotoxic, non-immune promotion mechanism for keratinocyte skin cancers (BCC/SCC). This matters because 5 of the 8 cancers were NMSC, in older/sun-exposed patients, two rated "Possibly Related," and concentrated in the higher-exposure 50 mg arm — a pattern consistent with a phototoxic contribution layered on top of the heavy background risk factors. It does not implicate the solid tumours (breast/prostate remain background on latency grounds), but it means the NMSC signal deserves more weight than the "no pathway to cancer" thesis allows.

The sponsor itself pre-flagged malignancy as an AESI

ABX464-104 protocol — verbatim
"Despite a lack/paucity of reported events in non-clinical and clinical trials, a potential impact on both innate and adaptive immune responses and the novel mechanism of action, could be a concern with regards to malignancies, mainly in the long-term. All malignancies including non-melanoma skin cancers will be reported as an AESI."

So the cancer cases are hitting a pre-specified Adverse Event of Special Interest that Abivax designated because of a theoretical long-term immune/MoA concern — which tempers the pure "background noise / nothing to see" framing on both sides of the debate.

miR-124 mechanistic direction — tumour suppressor, not promoter

The strongest reassuring mechanistic point (better than "it's not a kinase"): the specific miRNA obe raises, miR-124, is a tumour suppressor consistently silenced in cancers; restoring it is anti-proliferative/pro-apoptotic. Verified targets include STAT3, CDK6, PIK3CA, SLC16A1/MCT1, IL6R — i.e. obe pushes the dial in the anti-cancer direction systemically. [PMID 36573890 (MoA); 31819494 (melanoma); 27842510 (breast); 33333886 (CTCL)] This is a systemic argument and does not negate a local skin phototoxicity effect.

The sheet's pooled rates: 50 mg (588 PY): NMSC 0.85, non-NMSC 0.51, all-cancer-excl-dysplasia 1.36 / 100 PY; 25 mg (523 PY): NMSC 0.38, non-NMSC 0.00. The arithmetic is internally consistent and, pooled, both doses look "in range." Five flags:

1Optimistic patient-years. Maintenance is entered at 163/165 PY (= N × 44 wk, i.e. ~100% completion). True maintenance PY is ~135/142 (§3), so maintenance-window rates are understated ~15%. (The pooled numbers barely move — the OLE's ~325 PY dominates — so the "in range" pooled conclusion is robust; it's the maintenance-specific 50 mg signal that gets smoothed away.)
2Dose-attribution flatters 25 mg. The 6-K is explicit: Study 108's 130 patients took 50 mg for 2–4 years first, then de-escalated to 25 mg. Assigning their ~360 PY entirely to the "Pooled 25 mg" bucket (→ pristine 0.38/100 PY) buries years of prior 50 mg exposure where any latent risk would have accrued.
3Internal inconsistency. The study-level table shows ABTECT-1 induction NMSC = 1 with no matching case row, while the induction prostate case (row 10) is not reflected in the induction non-NMSC count.
4N overstates unique patients. "Pooled 50 mg N = 1048" double-counts the same people flowing induction → maintenance → OLE. Person-time pooling is defensible for an exposure-adjusted rate; the headline patient count is not.
5One factual error on labels. The thesis claims "no malignancy boxed warnings outside the JAK class." Anti-TNFs (infliximab, adalimumab) — both approved for UC — carry a boxed warning that explicitly includes malignancy/lymphoma. So malignancy boxed warnings exist in two UC classes (JAK and anti-TNF). It doesn't change the "no box for obe" conclusion — obe resembles neither — but it is a verifiable misstatement.

10 — Confidence, gaps & follow-up

Confident: the case disclosures are faithful; the biology (miR-124 tumour suppressor; non-immunosuppressant; not a kinase) makes drug-caused carcinogenesis implausible; short latencies make the two solid tumours almost certainly pre-existing; the verifiable Pathway-2 immunosuppression data point the right way; the JAK comparator genuinely carries a boxed malignancy warning obe is unlikely to inherit.

Over-claimed (by both company and bulls): the "within background" benchmark is built from immunosuppressant-treated cohorts and two weak/cherry-picked sources; on a clean maintenance-matched denominator the 50 mg (and pooled) rates exceed it; the dose-concentration is real and glossed.

Newly resolved (15 Jun): genotoxicity is now primary-sourced — Ames + micronucleus negative (ABX464-104 protocol). But the same document reveals a phototoxic metabolite (NMSC-relevant), a real 9-month cynomolgus immunosuppression signal, and the sponsor's own malignancy AESI — so the "no MoA for cancer" claim is over-broad; a local skin-promotion route is plausible.

Genuinely unresolved: (i) 2–4 events → CIs spanning the whole benchmark (underpowered); (ii) the 2-year carcinogenicity bioassays are still unconfirmed; (iii) the long-term OLE/Study-108 malignancy tally is asserted ("no new signals"), not quantified; (iv) whether the NMSC clustering reflects phototoxicity vs background risk factors.
